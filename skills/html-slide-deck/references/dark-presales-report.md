# Dark Presales Report Design Notes

Use this reference when creating dark-background presales report slides.

## Visual Positioning

- Audience: customer executives, sales leaders, presales architects, project stakeholders.
- Tone: professional, technical, confident, and restrained.
- Best fit: strategy explanation, architecture thinking, competitive analysis, roadmap, delivery plan, risk control.
- Avoid: marketing hero pages, decorative gradients as the main content, large empty slogans, excessive glow effects.

## Layout Rules

- Use a strong title and one concise subtitle at the top.
- Put the main argument on the left and evidence on the right when explaining strategy.
- Use cards sparingly: one card for a major argument, not nested cards.
- Screenshots and architecture diagrams should be large enough to inspect.
- Pull key conclusions out of screenshots into readable labels or notes.
- Include the standard page set when building a complete template: cover, agenda, three-category page, timeline page, one-sentence page, transition page, and closing page.

## Color Rules

- Base background: near-black blue.
- Main text: off-white.
- Secondary text: muted blue-gray.
- Accent blue/cyan: structure, section labels, lines.
- Gold: decision points, numbers, highlighted terms, or commercial emphasis.
- Avoid making the whole slide one-note blue; gold and green can create hierarchy.

## Typography Rules

- Keep Chinese titles bold and compact.
- Do not use viewport-width font scaling that makes text unstable.
- Do not shrink important content below presentation readability.
- Avoid negative letter spacing.

## Presales Content Pattern

For strategy pages, prefer this sequence:

1. Customer objective
2. Current blocker or competitive risk
3. Solution strategy
4. Evidence: screenshot, architecture, data, or benchmark
5. Action path: owner, timing, next step

For solution pages, prefer this sequence:

1. What problem this solves
2. Why this architecture is suitable
3. Where the differentiators are
4. How to implement
5. How to verify success

## Template Page Types

- Cover: deep blue technology style with institutional brand area, large center title, city or platform silhouette, and curved light bands.
- Agenda: four-part grid with strong numbering and compact descriptions.
- Three-category page: three equal columns for pain points, strategy, capability, product modules, or delivery outcomes.
- Timeline page: four-stage horizontal path for project plan, roadmap, implementation, review, or phased rollout.
- One-sentence page: one large statement for a key conclusion, principle, or transition.
- Transition page: large section title with a short supporting line.
- Closing page: centered closing statement.
